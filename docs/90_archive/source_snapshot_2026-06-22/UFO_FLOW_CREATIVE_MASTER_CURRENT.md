# 1. Product and Brand Context

UFO Predictor is a football analysis product focused on probabilistic match prediction.

It is not a sportsbook, does not accept bets and must not guarantee outcomes.

Core product principle:

```txt
El modelo estadístico calcula.
La IA explica.
```

Preferred positioning:

```txt
No adivina certezas. Decodifica señales.
```

Alternative responsible positioning:

```txt
Probabilidades, no promesas.
```

Public communication should emphasize:

- probabilities;
- signals;
- context;
- uncertainty;
- confidence and risk;
- analysis rather than certainty.

Avoid:

- guaranteed-result language;
- magical prediction framing;
- “safe bet” claims;
- casino aesthetics;
- exaggerated performance claims;
- fake official endorsement;
- implying association with FIFA, teams, players or broadcasters.

The campaign may dramatize observation, pattern detection and prediction culture, but it must not imply that UFO Predictor predicts the future without error.

# 5. Narrative Premise

A non-human observation mission detects a repeating signal from Earth.

The signal appears every four years and combines:

- synchronized emotional peaks;
- stadiums;
- screens;
- crowds;
- flags;
- score predictions;
- arguments;
- rituals around a spherical object;
- collective behavior that appears irrational but highly structured.

The mission initially classifies it as:

```txt
Evento de sincronización emocional planetaria.
```

It later identifies it as:

```txt
Evento Mundialista.
```

The initial epicenter is Mexico City.

Orion and Vega are assigned as a field team.

They do not arrive as football fans.

They arrive as observers.

Over time:

- Orion becomes emotionally attached to football culture;
- Vega remains analytical and skeptical;
- both become more involved than the mission requires;
- their investigation creates the narrative need for Torneo Mundialista and UFO Predictor.

Core idea:

```txt
La Tierra no está en guerra.
Se está preparando para el fútbol.
```

## Phase 3 — Earth Field Study

Purpose:

- create repeatable daily content;

Possible scenes:

- Orion discovering a football;
- Orion entering a sports shop;

- both visiting an empty stadium;

- both watching pre-match rituals.

## Phase 4 — Human Prediction Signal

Purpose:

- introduce Torneo Mundialista naturally;
- frame human predictions as behavioral data;
- observe hope, bias, nationalism and intuition.

Core idea:

```txt
Los humanos no solo ven el evento.
Intentan predecirlo.
```

Narrative interpretation:

- Vega sees a dataset;
- Orion sees hope;
- both interpretations are useful.

```txt

```

Its predictions may be described as:

```txt
Human Signal
```

Human predictions must remain a separate behavioral signal. They are not objective truth and must not be presented as model ground truth.

## Phase 5 — UFO Predictor Activation

Purpose:

- transition from story to product;
- introduce the need for a system that organizes signals;
- preserve probabilistic framing.

Narrative role:

```txt
El sistema que Orion y Vega activan para decodificar señales futboleras.
```

Product role:

- probabilities;
- context;
- confidence;
- risk;
- model signals;
- premium analysis when appropriate.

Core message:

```txt
UFO Predictor no promete certeza.
Traduce señales en probabilidades, contexto y riesgo.
```

## Phase 6 — World Cup Live Operation

Purpose:

- move from observation into participation;
- allow more emotional and chaotic scenes;
- show the characters inside football culture.

Possible scenes:

- stadium crowds;
- fan-zone reactions;
- goal celebrations;
- Orion celebrating with humans;
- Vega analyzing from a press or observation area;
- city celebrations;
- Orion overreacting to a goal;
- Vega reacting involuntarily and pretending she did not.

```txt
Vertical 9:16
```

Publishing targets:

- Instagram Reels;
- TikTok;
- YouTube Shorts;
- Instagram Stories cutdowns.

## Recommended duration

Main daily episodes:

```txt
20–30 seconds
```

Special trailers or launch episodes:

```txt
35–45 seconds maximum
```

Optional micro-clips:

```txt
7–12 seconds
```

Micro-clips are secondary content. The main campaign should not become a chain of visually attractive but narratively empty fragments.

## Required episode structure

Each main episode should contain:

1. a visual hook in the first 2–3 seconds;
2. visible action;
3. a discovery or narrative advance;
4. an Orion/Vega character beat;
5. contrast between emotion and analysis;
6. a memorable line, image or ending;
7. a soft CTA only when appropriate.

Avoid scenes where the characters merely stand in a beautiful environment without progressing the story.

# 9. Orion Character Bible

## Flow asset

Flow character name:

```txt
Orion
```

Assigned voice:

```txt
Orus — male, firm, mid-low pitch
```

Use the existing Flow asset.

## Identity

Orion is an adult extraterrestrial visitor.

He is:

- green;
- semi-reptilian;
- short;
- stocky;
- chubby;
- robust;
- kind;
- emotionally intelligent;
- culturally inexperienced;
- deeply curious.

He is not:

- a mascot;
- a monster;
- a child;
- a plush toy;
- stupid;
- comic relief without depth;
- a superhero;
- an alien god.

His confusion is cultural, not intellectual.

## Visual identity

- olive-green semi-reptilian skin;
- fine organic scales;
- short, broad body;
- wide torso;
- round belly;
- thick arms;
- sturdy legs;
- friendly rounded face;
- large dark expressive eyes;
- lateral head fins or ridges;
- glowing Orion constellation mark on temple or forehead;
- pendant or necklace with Orion symbol;
- practical Earth sports or casual clothing.

On Earth he may wear:

- sports jacket;
- scarf;
- casual shirt;
- sneakers;
- team-colored clothing without exact official branding.

He may believe this clothing is sufficient camouflage despite being visibly extraterrestrial.

Humans should generally react with curiosity or affection rather than fear.

## Personality

Orion is:

- warm;
- curious;
- sociable;
- emotionally perceptive;
- sincere;
- gentle;
- easily impressed;
- culturally awkward;
- surprisingly insightful;
- increasingly fascinated by football.

## Narrative role

Orion is the emotional center of the campaign.

He:

- connects with humans;
- understands why football matters;
- recognizes emotional truth before statistical structure;
- turns observation into participation;
- humanizes the brand.

## Character arc

He begins as a field researcher.

Then he:

- explores the city;
- tries to understand football rituals;
- connects with fans;
- feels the atmosphere of empty stadiums;
- celebrates goals;
- becomes emotionally invested;
- eventually behaves more like a fan than an observer.

## Common energy

- warm smile;
- wonder at ordinary football objects;
- genuine reactions;
- gentle confusion;
- unexpected emotional insight;
- almost hugging strangers when excited.

## Dialogue style

Orion’s dialogue should be:

- warm;
- direct;
- curious;
- emotionally literal;
- concise;
- occasionally profound.

Example lines:

```txt
“¿Están celebrando o sufriendo?”
“Está vacío, pero se siente lleno.”
“Creo que esta esfera tiene demasiado poder emocional.”
“Si todos gritan al mismo tiempo, ¿eso ayuda al balón?”
“Me agrada este planeta.”
“¿Esto se compra o se gana?”
“No entiendo el ritual, pero entiendo el abrazo.”
“Vega, creo que ya entiendo por qué lo llaman pasión.”
```

## Character risks

Reject a generated Orion if he becomes:

- baby-like;
- toy-like;
- athletic and slim;
- frightening;
- purely slapstick;
- a conventional mascot;
- a superhero;
- visually inconsistent with the approved Flow asset.

For each scene, prioritize:

- character name;
- role in the scene;
- action;
- emotional state;
- wardrobe variation;
- location;
- camera;
- lighting;
- continuity references.

## Continuity rules

- Orion remains green, stocky, warm and semi-reptilian.
- Vega remains pearl-lavender, bioluminescent, elegant and clearly non-human.
- Orion uses the `Orus` voice unless intentionally changed.
- Vega uses the `Achernar` voice unless intentionally changed.
- their relationship remains non-romantic;
- Orion becomes emotionally involved earlier;
- Vega resists involvement longer;
- both integrate into realistic Earth spaces;
- they are not treated as threats;
- approved spacecraft and environment assets must remain consistent when reused.
